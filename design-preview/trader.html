Trader · terminal

Persona. A power / carbon trader on the SA exchange. Decisions in sub-seconds, eyes across 4–6 monitors, hands on the keyboard — mouse is a last resort. Lives inside the order book, live positions, daily VaR & scenarios, position limits and counterparty margin. Away from the desk they need exactly two things: an alert feed and a one-thumb kill-switch / close-out. They tolerate density; they do not tolerate latency or a misread number.

Why three directions

A trader's screen is a personality choice. Some want maximum signal density (terminal), some want a calm keyboard-driven surface that disappears (command-palette), some manage by risk first and the book second (risk-radar). We bet all three and let the desk choose.
