The Regulator Bench

Authority, due process, and evidence — for the officer who adjudicates the market.
Persona

NERSA regulatory officer. Adjudicates licence applications, runs compliance inspections, resolves complaints, and signs determinations under ERA 2006 and the NERSA Grid Code. Every act must withstand appeal — due process and the evidentiary record are the job.

Environment: Desktop, dual-monitor, in chambers. Reads long dossiers; rarely on the move. Lives in an SLA-clocked inbox fed by every other role's escalations — levies, take-or-pay, curtailment claims, defaults, surveillance — all surface here.

Needs: A prioritised queue that never lets an SLA lapse, a certified file behind every decision, tamper-evident audit, and one-click certified exports for the gazette and the courts.

Design rationale

Formal, serif-tinged, ink-on-paper identity — this is a bench, not a trading floor. Calm authority, never playful, generous reading measure, certified seals as first-class UI.

Three bets for one officer's day: (1) the docket — triage the SLA queue; (2) the dossier — work one case as a tamper-evident certified file; (3) oversight — see the whole market's compliance posture and enforcement pipeline. Mobile collapses to triage + sign-off only.
