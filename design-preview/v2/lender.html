Lender

Project-finance credit desk · /lender-suite

A project-finance credit officer at a DFI or commercial debt fund, carrying a book of REIPPPP and merchant-IPP facilities. Works desktop-first, deliberately, analytically — long sessions across covenant certificates, DSCR tests, drawdown use-of-proceeds and DSRA/MRA reserve positions. Their stress lives entirely on the downside: a covenant breach, an event of default, step-in, write-off. What they need is not more dashboards — it's for the one facility that is slipping to announce itself, with the cure window already counting down.

Design rationale Three genuinely different bets on how risk reaches a credit officer: a portfolio heatmap that drills to the breach, a single-deal covenant waterfall for forensic depth, and a calm watchlist that stays quiet until a clock starts. All three obey one law — the at-risk covenant surfaces itself.
⏱

Ease, for a lender: the risk that matters finds you. Every option opens already pointed at the breaching covenant — its cure clock running, its waterfall failing — so you never hunt through a table to learn what is about to default.

Accessibility & identity notes

Palette is parchment/ink ledger, never AI-purple. State colour is reinforced by text labels and shape (tick, pill, node) — never colour alone. Text-on-fill pairs meet WCAG AA: white on --red #a3231f ≈ 6.4:1, ink on --paper ≈ 14:1, --amber #9a6310 on amber-bg ≈ 5.1:1. All buttons and nav items are ≥24px tap height. Cure clocks use monospace for glanceable countdown. Tabs use role="tablist" with aria-selected.
