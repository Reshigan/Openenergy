⬡ Support / OEM · Field O&M + Service Desk

Built for gloves, glare & dead zones.

Persona

Thabo runs O&M across a cluster of PV plants and BESS sites in the Northern Cape. He works outdoors in full sun, wearing gloves, climbing combiner racks with a phone in one hand. Signal drops the moment he's inside a transformer kiosk. He has zero patience for typing, login walls, or a desktop UI shrunk onto glass. Back at the desk, a dispatcher triages the P1–P4 queue and routes him.

Design rationale

One mobile-first hero per option, three different bets on "the next job": a one-thumb swipe queue, a map-routed drive plan, and an offline-first checklist. All survive no-signal, all use 56px tap targets, none require typing to close a job. Desktop is the dispatcher's triage board.

THEME · EASE For a gloved field tech, ease = the next job in one thumb, works offline, no typing. Every option below names the one thing it makes effortless.

Ease ⟶ Closing a work-order never needs a connection — tick the steps offline; the sync ledger sends the evidence chain the instant signal returns.
