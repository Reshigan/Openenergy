CEC · System Operator Desk

Grid Operator / Dispatcher

SAST 14:37:08 · MTU 59 · f = 49.98 Hz

Who: A real-time system operator at the national control centre — the dispatcher who keeps supply and demand balanced across the transmission network second by second. Environment: a multi-screen control room, wall-board to the left, telemetry never sleeping. Device: wide desktop by default; a hardened phone PWA for the floor-walk and after-hours standby. What they need: to see the network state spatially and act on a constraint the instant it appears — issue a curtailment, accept a dispatch nomination, clear an N-1 violation — without ever leaving the picture in front of them.

Why these three bets: a dispatcher is a spatial, real-time, constraint-driven thinker. So we explore the three native mental models — the network map, the dispatch-stack timeline, and the constraint-alarm board — and in every one the operator can resolve the constraint in place, where they already see it.
Theme · Ease For a dispatcher, ease means no context-switching: the constraint, its evidence, and the action that clears it all live in one glance and one click — on the surface where it surfaced.
